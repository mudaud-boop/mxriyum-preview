a note from the kitchen —

Hi, I'm Mariyum.

I started mxriyum in 2021 as a place to keep track of what I was cooking. Four years and a few hundred recipes later, it's grown into something closer to a journal — a running love letter to slow mornings, Sunday breads, and dessert for no occasion at all.

flour in my hair,
as usual —

Ilearned to cook from my mother, who learned from hers, and who taught me that dinner is a conversation. That every dish is someone's story, usually passed down in approximations — "a little of this, until it smells right." I've tried to write it all down.

This site is where I keep the recipes I come back to. Some are family, some are inventions, most are somewhere in between. I test everything at home, in a kitchen that is often too small, with light that is sometimes good and sometimes not. The photos are mine. The sketches are mine too, drawn in the margins of notebooks while bread is rising.

cooking is the best love letter.

If you make something here and love it, tell me. If you change it — and you will — tell me that too. The best recipes are the ones that get handed around, changed a little, and handed on.

— Mariyum
