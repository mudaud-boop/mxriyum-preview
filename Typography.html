Ingredients.
Medjool dates, pitted and roughly chopped. Boiling water poured over them until they sigh. Baking soda — always the cheap trick — to help the dates slump into paste. Brown butter, cooled just enough to still pour. Muscovado sugar, eggs, flour, a big pinch of salt, and one vanilla bean if the pantry is feeling generous.
Makes one 8-inch cake. Keeps, loosely wrapped, for three days.

Rules.

The invisible rules that make type feel intentional. Break them only when you mean to.
01
Serif above 20, sans below.
Cormorant's brackets need room. At <20px, switch to Inter so letterforms stay crisp. Use var(--t-h4) as the cutoff.
02
Display weight = 300.
Large serif text (40px+) always uses weight 300. The generous sizing gives weight; a bolder cut looks clumsy next to the script accent.
03
Tighten as you grow.
Negative letter-spacing on headlines: −0.01em at h3, −0.02em at h2+, −0.025em on display. Body stays at 0.
04
Line-height shrinks with size.
Display: 0.95. H1–H2: 1.05. H3–H4: 1.15–1.35. Body: 1.65. Caption/eyebrow: 1.5.
05
Measure 45–75 characters.
Body paragraphs cap at ~60ch. Anything wider is hard to read; anything narrower breaks rhythm. Use max-width: 56ch.
06
Script sparingly.
Pinyon appears once, maybe twice per page. It's a signature — the moment it repeats, it stops feeling special.
